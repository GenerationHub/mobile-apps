Privacy Policy — Lock Screen Notes

Last updated: July 22, 2026

Lock Screen Notes (“the app”, “we”, “us”) lets you pin notes to your Lock Screen and Home Screen widgets. This policy explains what data the app handles.

Data stored on your device

Your notes, todos, and design choices are stored locally on your device (in a shared app group so the widget can display them). We do not operate servers that store your note content, and you do not need an account to use the app.

Purchases

In-app purchases are processed by Apple and by our payments provider, RevenueCat. RevenueCat receives a non-identifying, randomly generated app user ID and your purchase status so purchases can be restored across your devices. We do not receive your name, email, or payment card details. See RevenueCat’s privacy policy and Apple’s privacy policy.

Analytics

We use PostHog to collect anonymous, product-usage events (for example, which screens are opened and whether a design pack was unlocked) so we can improve the app. This data is tied to a random identifier, not to your name or email. See PostHog’s privacy policy.

Advertising & measurement

We use the Meta (Facebook) SDK to measure the performance of advertising campaigns that promote the app. With your permission (requested through Apple’s App Tracking Transparency prompt), this may include your device’s advertising identifier and may be used to track installs and in-app events across apps and websites owned by other companies, for advertising and measurement. If you decline the tracking prompt, this identifier is not used. You can change your choice anytime in Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking. See Meta’s privacy policy.

Children’s privacy

The app is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect personal information from them.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by the “Last updated” date above.
