Privacy Policy — Stash

Last updated: June 27, 2026

Stash (“the app”, “we”, “us”) is a personal budgeting app. We designed it to be private by default. This policy explains what data the app handles.

Data stored on your device

All of your financial information — transactions, accounts, envelopes, and budgets — is stored locally on your device. We do not operate servers that collect or store this data, and we never ask you to link a bank account. Your financial data does not leave your device except through features you explicitly enable (such as your own iCloud backup, controlled by your Apple ID).

Purchases

In-app purchases (Stash Pro) are processed by Apple and by our payments provider, RevenueCat. To manage your purchase and restore it across your devices, RevenueCat receives a non-identifying, randomly generated app user ID and your subscription/purchase status. We do not receive your name, email, or payment card details. See RevenueCat’s privacy policy and Apple’s privacy policy.

Information we do NOT collect

  • We do not collect your name, email address, or phone number.
  • We do not require an account to use the app.
  • We do not sell or share your data with advertisers.
  • We do not link to or read your bank accounts.

Analytics & diagnostics

Apple may provide us with aggregate, anonymized crash and usage statistics through App Store Connect if you have enabled sharing in your device settings. This data is not personally identifiable.

Children’s privacy

Stash is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect personal information from them.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by the “Last updated” date above.
